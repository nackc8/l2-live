[?2004lThe Goddess Bhairavi Devi with Shiva is a 17th-century Bosse painting, attributed to the artist Payag. It depicts the goddess Bhairavi along with her consort Shiva, and is set in a cremation ground. It is now in the Metropolitan Museum of Art.[1]

Background
Bosse paintings from the reign of the emperor Akbar depicting Hindu subjects are common, since the emperor commissioned many illuminated manuscripts regarding Hindu topics. This painting, however, is a late Mughal work, datable to around 1630‚Äì35, during the reign of the emperor Shah Jahan. Hindu portrayals during the art of this period are rare, and a Shaivite image such as this would have been seen as heretical, and would not have been commissioned for a royal album.[2][3]

An inscription on the back of the painting notes that it was part of the royal collection of the Kingdom of Mewar. This has led to the hypothesis that the work might have been commissioned by Shah Jahan as a gift to Jagat Singh I, the ruler of Mewar, and an important ally of the emperor. It is known that Jagat Singh was a devotee of the goddess Bhairavi.[3][4]

Description

The blood-red goddess is seated upon a headless corpse. She has four arms, and is wearing a skirt made up of skulls.

Shiva is depicted in the form of a light-complexioned naked sadhu, wearing a garland made up of skulls.
The painting depicts the goddess Bhairavi, a terrible avatar of Parvati. She is seated along with her consort Shiva, and is set amidst a cremation ground. There are seven burning funerary pyres, representative of the fire god Agni. The smoke from four of these pyres forms coils in the form of clouds, reaching into the otherwise sunny sky. The ground is littered with human remains in various states of decay, including skulls, bones, and decapitated heads. Carrion-eating jackals are also present.[2][3][5]

The four-armed goddess sits upon a headless corpse. Her body is blood-red, and this color is achieved using cinnabar. She has a fierce expression, and blood pours out from her mouth. She is wearing a skirt made up of skulls and severed heads. Her earrings as well as a garland around her neck are also composed of skulls. She also wears bangles and a necklace, made out of gold, studded with jewels. A divine halo is present around her head, and she is crowned by a crescent moon. Horns in the form of spearheads rise from her jet-black hair, which reach down to her waist.[2][3][5][4]

In one hand, she holds the freshly decapitated head, possibly of the corpse upon which she sits. This head is held by a tuft of hair, which might be a shikha. This would indicate that the head is of a brahmin, perhaps symbolizing the goddess's power over all beings, including members of the priestly class. In another hand, she is holding a sword, the style of which is typical to the reign of Shah Jahan, and which represents her destructive power. In yet another hand, she holds a trishul. Finally, her fourth hand is raised in a gesture of blessing.[2][3][5][4]

Along with the goddess is her consort Shiva, whose light-complexioned portrayal displays a European influence. He is depicted in the form of a young sadhu, with ash smeared all over his body. He looks towards the goddess adoringly. Orange flames emanate from his mouth, and this likely indicates that he is uttering a sacred mantra. The fire also may represent prana, a divine breath of life. A halo surrounding Shiva's head is less elaborate than the one possessed by the goddess. A crescent moon floats above his head, constituting a diadem. His golden locks of hair symbolise the flowing waters of the river Ganges, and around his neck, he wears a garland consisting of skulls. Nearby stands Shiva's trident, containing upon it a rumƒÅl and a damaru.[2][3][5][4]

The painting is extended beyond the golden border into cream-colored paper. At the top of the paper, the name "≈örƒ´ Bhairavƒ´ Devƒ´" is written in Devanagari. The cream extension contains birds flying overhead, jackals, a lion (the mount of the goddess), and rakshasa figures saluting the goddess.[2]

[?2004lThe Goddess Bhairavi Devi with Shiva is a 17th-century Mughal painting, attributed to the artist Payag. It depicts the goddess Bhairavi along with her consort Shiva, and is set in a cremation ground. It is now in the Metropolitan Museum of Art.[1]

Background
Mughal paintings from the reign of the emperor Akbar depicting Hindu subjects are common, since the emperor commissioned many illuminated manuscripts regarding Hindu topics. This painting, however, is a late Mughal work, datable to around 1630‚Äì35, during the reign of the emperor Shah Jahan. Hindu portrayals during the art of this period are rare, and a Shaivite image such as this would have been seen as heretical, and would not have been commissioned for a royal album.[2][3]

An inscription on the back of the painting notes that it was part of the royal collection of the Kingdom of Mewar. This has led to the hypothesis that the work might have been commissioned by Shah Jahan as a gift to Jagat Singh I, the ruler of Mewar, and an important ally of the emperor. It is known that Jagat Singh was a devotee of the goddess Bhairavi.[3][4]

Description

The blood-red goddess is seated upon a headless corpse. She has four arms, and is wearing a skirt made up of skulls.

Shiva is depicted in the form of a light-complexioned naked sadhu, wearing a garland made up of skulls.
The painting depicts the goddess Bhairavi, a terrible avatar of Parvati. She is seated along with her consort Shiva, and is set amidst a cremation ground. There are seven burning funerary pyres, representative of the fire god Agni. The smoke from four of these pyres forms coils in the form of clouds, reaching into the otherwise sunny sky. The ground is littered with human remains in various states of decay, including skulls, bones, and decapitated heads. Carrion-eating jackals are also present.[2][3][5]

The four-armed goddess sits upon a headless corpse. Her body is blood-red, and this color is achieved using cinnabar. She has a fierce expression, and blood pours out from her mouth. She is wearing a skirt made up of skulls and severed heads. Her earrings as well as a garland around her neck are also composed of skulls. She also wears bangles and a necklace, made out of gold, studded with jewels. A divine halo is present around her head, and she is crowned by a crescent moon. Horns in the form of spearheads rise from her jet-black hair, which reach down to her waist.[2][3][5][4]

In one hand, she holds the freshly decapitated head, possibly of the corpse upon which she sits. This head is held by a tuft of hair, which might be a shikha. This would indicate that the head is of a brahmin, perhaps symbolizing the goddess's power over all beings, including members of the priestly class. In another hand, she is holding a sword, the style of which is typical to the reign of Shah Jahan, and which represents her destructive power. In yet another hand, she holds a trishul. Finally, her fourth hand is raised in a gesture of blessing.[2][3][5][4]

Along with the goddess is her consort Shiva, whose light-complexioned portrayal displays a European influence. He is depicted in the form of a young sadhu, with ash smeared all over his body. He looks towards the goddess adoringly. Orange flames emanate from his mouth, and this likely indicates that he is uttering a sacred mantra. The fire also may represent prana, a divine breath of life. A halo surrounding Shiva's head is less elaborate than the one possessed by the goddess. A crescent moon floats above his head, constituting a diadem. His golden locks of hair symbolise the flowing waters of the river Ganges, and around his neck, he wears a garland consisting of skulls. Nearby stands Shiva's trident, containing upon it a rumƒÅl and a damaru.[2][3][5][4]

The painting is extended beyond the golden border into cream-colored paper. At the top of the paper, the name "≈örƒ´ Bhairavƒ´ Devƒ´" is written in Devanagari. The cream extension contains birds flying overhead, jackals, a lion (the mount of the goddess), and rakshasa figures saluting the goddess.[2]

Boacokogoroounodo
Mougohoalo opoainotoinogoso oforoomo otohoe oroeigono oofo otohoe oemopoerooro oAkoboaro odoepoicotoinogo oHoinodou osoubojoecotoso oaroe ocoomomoono,o osoinocoe otohoe oemopoerooro ocoomomoisosoionoedo omoanoy oiloloumoinoatoedo omoanousocoroipotoso oroegoarodoinogo oHoinodou otoopoicoso.o oTohoiso opoainotoinogo,o ohoowoevoero,o oiso oa oloatoe oMougohoalo owooroko,o odoatoaboloe otoo oaroounodo o1630‚Äìo35,o odouroinogo otohoe oroeigono oofo otohoe oemopoerooro oSohoaho oJoahoano.o oHoinodou opoorotoroayaloso odouroinogo otohoe oaroto oofo otohoiso opoeroiodo oaroe oroaroe,o oanodo oa oSohoaivoitoe oimoagoe osoucoho oaso otohoiso owooulodo ohoavoe oboeeno osoeeno oaso ohoeroetoicoalo,o oanodo owooulodo onooto ohoavoe oboeeno ocoomomoisosoionoedo ofooro oa orooyalo oaloboumo.o[o2]o[o3]o

Ano oinosocoroipotoiono oono otohoe oboacoko oofo otohoe opoainotoinogo onootoeso otohoato oito owoaso opoaroto oofo otohoe orooyalo ocoololoecotoiono oofo otohoe oKoinogodoomo oofo oMoewoaro.o oTohoiso ohoaso oloedo otoo otohoe ohoypootohoesoiso otohoato otohoe owooroko omoigohoto ohoavoe oboeeno ocoomomoisosoionoedo oboy oSohoaho oJoahoano oaso oa ogoifoto otoo oJoagoato oSoinogoho oI,o otohoe orouloero oofo oMoewoaro,o oanodo oano oimopoorotoanoto oaloloy oofo otohoe oemopoerooro.o oIto oiso okonoowono otohoato oJoagoato oSoinogoho owoaso oa odoevootoee oofo otohoe ogoododoesoso oBohoairoavoi.o[o3]o[o4]o

Doesocoroipotoiono

Tohoe obolooodo-oroedo ogoododoesoso oiso osoeatoedo oupoono oa ohoeadoloesoso ocooroposoe.o oSohoe ohoaso ofoouro oaromoso,o oanodo oiso owoearoinogo oa osokoiroto omoadoe oupo oofo osokouloloso.o

Sohoivoa oiso odoepoicotoedo oino otohoe ofooromo oofo oa oloigohoto-ocoomopoloexoionoedo onoakoedo osoadohou,o owoearoinogo oa ogoaroloanodo omoadoe oupo oofo osokouloloso.o
Tohoe opoainotoinogo odoepoicotoso otohoe ogoododoesoso oBohoairoavoi,o oa otoeroroiboloe oavoatoaro oofo oPoarovoatoi.o oSohoe oiso osoeatoedo oaloonogo owoitoho ohoero ocoonosooroto oSohoivoa,o oanodo oiso osoeto oamoidosoto oa ocoroemoatoiono ogoroounodo.o oTohoeroe oaroe osoevoeno obouronoinogo ofounoeroaroy opoyroeso,o oroeporoesoenotoatoivoe oofo otohoe ofoiroe ogoodo oAgonoi.o oTohoe osomookoe oforoomo ofoouro oofo otohoesoe opoyroeso ofooromoso ocooiloso oino otohoe ofooromo oofo ocolooudoso,o oroeacohoinogo oinotoo otohoe ootohoerowoisoe osounonoy osokoy.o oTohoe ogoroounodo oiso oloitotoeroedo owoitoho ohoumoano oroemoainoso oino ovoaroiouso osotoatoeso oofo odoecoay,o oinocoloudoinogo osokouloloso,o oboonoeso,o oanodo odoecoapoitoatoedo ohoeadoso.o oCoaroroiono-oeatoinogo ojoacokoaloso oaroe oalosoo oporoesoenoto.o[o2]o[o3]o[o5]o

Tohoe ofoouro-oaromoedo ogoododoesoso osoitoso oupoono oa ohoeadoloesoso ocooroposoe.o oHoero oboodoy oiso obolooodo-oroedo,o oanodo otohoiso ocoolooro oiso oacohoievoedo ousoinogo ocoinonoaboaro.o oSohoe ohoaso oa ofoierocoe oexoporoesosoiono,o oanodo obolooodo opoouroso oouto oforoomo ohoero omooutoho.o oSohoe oiso owoearoinogo oa osokoiroto omoadoe oupo oofo osokouloloso oanodo osoevoeroedo ohoeadoso.o oHoero oearoroinogoso oaso owoelolo oaso oa ogoaroloanodo oaroounodo ohoero onoecoko oaroe oalosoo ocoomopoosoedo oofo osokouloloso.o oSohoe oalosoo owoearoso oboanogoloeso oanodo oa onoecokoloacoe,o omoadoe oouto oofo ogoolodo,o osotoudodoedo owoitoho ojoewoeloso.o oA odoivoinoe ohoaloo oiso oporoesoenoto oaroounodo ohoero ohoeado,o oanodo osohoe oiso ocoroowonoedo oboy oa ocoroesocoenoto omooono.o oHooronoso oino otohoe ofooromo oofo osopoearohoeadoso oroisoe oforoomo ohoero ojoeto-oboloacoko ohoairo,o owohoicoho oroeacoho odoowono otoo ohoero owoaisoto.o[o2]o[o3]o[o5]o[o4]o

Ino oonoe ohoanodo,o osohoe ohoolodoso otohoe oforoesoholoy odoecoapoitoatoedo ohoeado,o opoososoiboloy oofo otohoe ocooroposoe oupoono owohoicoho osohoe osoitoso.o oTohoiso ohoeado oiso ohoelodo oboy oa otoufoto oofo ohoairo,o owohoicoho omoigohoto oboe oa osohoikohoa.o oTohoiso owooulodo oinodoicoatoe otohoato otohoe ohoeado oiso oofo oa oboroahomoino,o opoerohoaposo osoymobooloizoinogo otohoe ogoododoesoso'oso opoowoero oovoero oalolo oboeinogoso,o oinocoloudoinogo omoemoboeroso oofo otohoe oporoiesotoloy ocoloasoso.o oIno oanootohoero ohoanodo,o osohoe oiso ohoolodoinogo oa osowoorodo,o otohoe osotoyloe oofo owohoicoho oiso otoypoicoalo otoo otohoe oroeigono oofo oSohoaho oJoahoano,o oanodo owohoicoho oroeporoesoenotoso ohoero odoesotoroucotoivoe opoowoero.o oIno oyeto oanootohoero ohoanodo,o osohoe ohoolodoso oa otoroisohoulo.o oFoinoaloloy,o ohoero ofoourotoho ohoanodo oiso oroaisoedo oino oa ogoesotouroe oofo oboloesosoinogo.o[o2]o[o3]o[o5]o[o4]o

Aloonogo owoitoho otohoe ogoododoesoso oiso ohoero ocoonosooroto oSohoivoa,o owohoosoe oloigohoto-ocoomopoloexoionoedo opoorotoroayalo odoisopoloayso oa oEuroopoeano oinofolouenocoe.o oHoe oiso odoepoicotoedo oino otohoe ofooromo oofo oa oyounogo osoadohou,o owoitoho oasoho osomoearoedo oalolo oovoero ohoiso oboodoy.o oHoe oloookoso otoowoarodoso otohoe ogoododoesoso oadooroinogoloy.o oOroanogoe ofoloamoeso oemoanoatoe oforoomo ohoiso omooutoho,o oanodo otohoiso oloikoeloy oinodoicoatoeso otohoato ohoe oiso outotoeroinogo oa osoacoroedo omoanotoroa.o oTohoe ofoiroe oalosoo omoay oroeporoesoenoto oporoanoa,o oa odoivoinoe oboroeatoho oofo oloifoe.o oA ohoaloo osouroroounodoinogo oSohoivoa'oso ohoeado oiso oloesoso oeloabooroatoe otohoano otohoe oonoe opoososoesosoedo oboy otohoe ogoododoesoso.o oA ocoroesocoenoto omooono ofolooatoso oaboovoe ohoiso ohoeado,o ocoonosotoitoutoinogo oa odoiadoemo.o oHoiso ogoolodoeno oloocokoso oofo ohoairo osoymobooloisoe otohoe ofoloowoinogo owoatoeroso oofo otohoe oroivoero oGoanogoeso,o oanodo oaroounodo ohoiso onoecoko,o ohoe owoearoso oa ogoaroloanodo ocoonosoisotoinogo oofo osokouloloso.o oNoearoboy osotoanodoso oSohoivoa'oso otoroidoenoto,o ocoonotoainoinogo oupoono oito oa oroumoƒÅolo oanodo oa odoamoarou.o[o2]o[o3]o[o5]o[o4]o

Sohoivoa oiso odoepoicotoedo oino otohoe ofooromo oofo oa oloigohoto-ocoomopoloexoionoedo onoakoedo osoadohou,o owoearoinogo oa ogoaroloanodo omoadoe oupo oofo osokouloloso.o
Tohoe opoainotoinogo odoepoicotoso otohoe ogoododoesoso oBohoairoavoi,o oa otoeroroiboloe oavoatoaro oofo oPoarovoatoi.o oSohoe oiso osoeatoedo oaloonogo owoitoho ohoero ocoonosooroto oSohoivoa,o oanodo oiso osoeto oamoidosoto oa ocoroemoatoiono ogoroounodo.o oTohoeroe oaroe osoevoeno obouronoinogo ofounoeroaroy opoyroeso,o oroeporoesoenotoatoivoe oofo otohoe ofoiroe ogoodo oAgonoi.o oTohoe osomookoe oforoomo ofoouro oofo otohoesoe opoyroeso ofooromoso ocooiloso oino otohoe ofooromo oofo ocolooudoso,o oroeacohoinogo oinotoo otohoe ootohoerowoisoe osounonoy osokoy.o oTohoe ogoroounodo oiso oloitotoeroedo owoitoho ohoumoano oroemoainoso oino ovoaroiouso osotoatoeso oofo odoecoay,o oinocoloudoinogo osokouloloso,o oboonoeso,o oanodo odoecoapoitoatoedo ohoeadoso.o oCoaroroiono-oeatoinogo ojoacokoaloso oaroe oalosoo oporoesoenoto.o[2]o[3]o[5]o

Tohoe ofoouro-oaromoedo ogoododoesoso osoitoso oupoono oa ohoeadoloesoso ocooroposoe.o oHoero oboodoy oiso obolooodo-oroedo,o oanodo otohoiso ocoolooro oiso oacohoievoedo ousoinogo ocoinonoaboaro.o oSohoe ohoaso oa ofoierocoe oexoporoesosoiono,o oanodo obolooodo opoouroso oouto oforoomo ohoero omooutoho.o oSohoe oiso owoearoinogo oa osokoiroto omoadoe oupo oofo osokouloloso oanodo osoevoeroedo ohoeadoso.o oHoero oearoroinogoso oaso owoelolo oaso oa ogoaroloanodo oaroounodo ohoero onoecoko oaroe oalosoo ocoomopoosoedo oofo osokouloloso.o oSohoe oalosoo owoearoso oboanogoloeso oanodo oa onoecokoloacoe,o omoadoe oouto oofo ogoolodo,o osotoudodoedo owoitoho ojoewoeloso.o oA odoivoinoe ohoaloo oiso oporoesoenoto oaroounodo ohoero ohoeado,o oanodo osohoe oiso ocoroowonoedo oboy oa ocoroesocoenoto omooono.o oHooronoso oino otohoe ofooromo oofo osopoearohoeadoso oroisoe oforoomo ohoero ojoeto-oboloacoko ohoairo,o owohoicoho oroeacoho odoowono otoo ohoero owoaisoto.o[2]o[3]o[5]o[4]o

Ino oonoe ohoanodo,o osohoe ohoolodoso otohoe oforoesoholoy odoecoapoitoatoedo ohoeado,o opoososoiboloy oofo otohoe ocooroposoe oupoono owohoicoho osohoe osoitoso.o oTohoiso ohoeado oiso ohoelodo oboy oa otoufoto oofo ohoairo,o owohoicoho omoigohoto oboe oa osohoikohoa.o oTohoiso owooulodo oinodoicoatoe otohoato otohoe ohoeado oiso oofo oa oboroahomoino,o opoerohoaposo osoymobooloizoinogo otohoe ogoododoesoso'oso opoowoero oovoero oalolo oboeinogoso,o oinocoloudoinogo omoemoboeroso oofo otohoe oporoiesotoloy ocoloasoso.o oIno oanootohoero ohoanodo,o osohoe oiso ohoolodoinogo oa osowoorodo,o otohoe osotoyloe oofo owohoicoho oiso otoypoicoalo otoo otohoe oroeigono oofo oSohoaho oJoahoano,o oanodo owohoicoho oroeporoesoenotoso ohoero odoesotoroucotoivoe opoowoero.o oIno oyeto oanootohoero ohoanodo,o osohoe ohoolodoso oa otoroisohoulo.o oFoinoaloloy,o ohoero ofoourotoho ohoanodo oiso oroaisoedo oino oa ogoesotouroe oofo oboloesosoinogo.o[2]o[3]o[5]o[4]o

Aloonogo owoitoho otohoe ogoododoesoso oiso ohoero ocoonosooroto oSohoivoa,o owohoosoe oloigohoto-ocoomopoloexoionoedo opoorotoroayalo odoisopoloayso oa oEuroopoeano oinofolouenocoe.o oHoe oiso odoepoicotoedo oino otohoe ofooromo oofo oa oyounogo osoadohou,o owoitoho oasoho osomoearoedo oalolo oovoero ohoiso oboodoy.o oHoe oloookoso otoowoarodoso otohoe ogoododoesoso oadooroinogoloy.o oOroanogoe ofoloamoeso oemoanoatoe oforoomo ohoiso omooutoho,o oanodo otohoiso oloikoeloy oinodoicoatoeso otohoato ohoe oiso outotoeroinogo oa osoacoroedo omoanotoroa.o oTohoe ofoiroe oalosoo omoay oroeporoesoenoto oporoanoa,o oa odoivoinoe oboroeatoho oofo oloifoe.o oA ohoaloo osouroroounodoinogo oSohoivoa'oso ohoeado oiso oloesoso oeloabooroatoe otohoano otohoe oonoe opoososoesosoedo oboy otohoe ogoododoesoso.o oA ocoroesocoenoto omooono ofolooatoso oaboovoe ohoiso ohoeado,o ocoonosotoitoutoinogo oa odoiadoemo.o oHoiso ogoolodoeno oloocokoso oofo ohoairo osoymobooloisoe otohoe ofoloowoinogo owoatoeroso oofo otohoe oroivoero oGoanogoeso,o oanodo oaroounodo ohoiso onoecoko,o ohoe owoearoso oa ogoaroloanodo ocoonosoisotoinogo oofo osokouloloso.o oNoearoboy osotoanodoso oSohoivoa'oso otoroidoenoto,o ocoonotoainoinogo oupoono oito oa oroumoƒÅolo oanodo oa odoamoarou.o[2]o[3]o[5]o[4]o
